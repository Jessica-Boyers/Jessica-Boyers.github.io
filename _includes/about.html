About

I am an engineer, maker, and entrepreneur in the Research Triangle of North Carolina! I got my master's degree in mechanical engineering from the Rochester Institute of Technology with a focus on additive manufacturing and design optimization. The bulk of my career has been spent in medical device R&D, designing innovative surgical tools for Teleflex Medical. I was the technical lead for several successful product development projects and have a few patents.

I have also always had an interest in using my engineering and maker skills to pursue artistic projects and self expression. Currently I am in the process of designing and building a life-size version of the classic game of Operation at an up-and-coming interactive art space in Durham called Future Hooman! In addition to my own exhibit, I provide engineering guidance to other artists creating electrical or mechanical exhibits, and I also coordinate our fire and electronics safety plans and procedures, including making sure everyone's electronics follow best safety practices.

I've always been a maker and had many ideas so at an opportune moment at the end of 2024 I left the corporate R&D world to start my own small business, Sovereigness Designs, creating custom designs, devices, and decor. The vision for Sovereigness Designs is to be a sort of ultra-minimized highly customizable rapid product development partner to help make people's ideas a reality or provide tight solutions to niche customers who are underserved by industrial markets. Theres a wide problem space where the solution is simple and straight forward with any access to common industrial solutions but infeasible for a weekend project handyman. With new capabilities in 3D printing, the democratization of coding with AI, and the commodification of high power motors, batteries, and electronics, many one-off problems considered too small to support a commercial market are easily solved by a single person or small team with the right experience and skillset - Sovereigness Designs aims to be that partner to address that problem space.
